Quick Guide: Using the Malaria Species Detector

Follow these simple steps to analyze your blood smear images.

1

Prepare Your Image

Ensure you have a clear, well-focused digital image of a Giemsa-stained thin blood smear. The image should primarily show red blood cells and any suspected parasites. Supported formats: JPG, JPEG, PNG.

For detailed smear preparation, see: CDC Guidelines.

2

Upload to the Tool

  1. Go to the Home page.
  2. Click the " Choose Image" button.
  3. Select your prepared image file from your computer.
  4. Click the "Analyze Image" button.
3

Review Prediction

The results page will show:

  • Your uploaded image.
  • Predicted Species: The model's top guess (e.g., Falciparum, Malariae, Ovale).
  • Confidence Score: How sure the model is about its top guess (0-100%). A higher score indicates more certainty from the model's perspective.
  • Class Probabilities: The deep learning model's confidence for each possible species.
4

Important Considerations

  • Image Quality: Poor quality images (blurry, bad staining, debris) will lead to unreliable predictions.
  • Purpose: This tool assists in potential species identification for research or education. It is NOT a diagnostic tool.
  • Limitations: The deep learning model is trained on specific data and may not recognize all parasite variations or co-infections perfectly.
